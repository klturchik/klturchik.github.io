Red Dwarf

About the project
Tools: Unity, C#, GIT, Visual Studio, SteamVR, VRTK, Blender, HTC Vive

Red Dwarf is a Virtual Reality space shooter game developed for the HTC Vive. The player controls their spaceship from inside the cockpit, with immersive VR-emulated flightstick controls. The game currently plays in an arcade-style, with the objective being to reach the highest score possible before dying. Inspirations for the game include old school space-sims such as Freelancer and Elite Dangerous as well as roguelikes such as FTL.

Contributions
I worked with a team of 3 other engineers and 2 3D artists to create Red Dwarf.
My contributions include:
  • Semi-newtonian physics based controls (keyboard & Vive)
  • 360 degree gyroscopic radar system
  • World space UI displays (game scene)
  • Spline based AI pathing
  • Targetting and hit detection for weapons
  • Destruction particle effects
  • Game Design (UI, weapon mechanics, interactions)
  • Project Management (Agile using Trello and Github)
What I Learned

While working on Red Dwarf I learned more about worldspace UI and developing controls for multiple devices (keyboard & Vive) simultaneously. The 3D nature of the game taught me a great deal about vector math as well. This was also a larger project with many components that required me to learn more about concise object-oriented architecture design.
One of our greatest challenges was making player controls feel intuitive and responsive while also alleviating motion sickness in VR. I designed and implemented the gyroscopic radar system and realistic controls with haptic feedback (using VRTK), which proved to significantly reduce motion sickness.
As this was my first indie game project I learned a great deal about game design and teamwork as well. My team followed Agile development practices using Trello and Github to coordinate tasks and we all participated in each stage of the game design process.

InVRasion

Senior Project

Previous Next
About the project
Tools: Unity, C#, GIT, Visual Studio, SteamVR, VRTK, Blender, HTC Vive, PSVR

InVRasion is an asynchronous multiplayer game that brings together keyboard/mouse and Virtual Reality gamers in an epic boss battle. An HTC Vive or PSVR player plays as a giant alien mech, while up to 4 split-screen players play as different classes of super soldiers who must coordinate their attacks to defeat the alien threat before it destroys an objective. Inspirations for the game include Pacific Rim, Attack on Titan, and many popular VR games.

Contributions
I worked with another engineer, a designer, and a 3D artist to create inVRasion.
My contributions include:
  • Projectile spawning via object pools
  • Wall running and grapple mechanics
  • Various weapon mechanics
  • World space UI displays
  • Keyboard/Mouse player controls
  • Game Design (UI, abilities & mechanics, goals, interactions)
  • Project Management (Agile using Trello and Github)
What I Learned

InVRasion was originally developed as my Senior Project for my Computer Science degree at Cal Poly Pomona. The project was very open ended, allowing me to experience the process of concepting a game, prototyping it, and then turning it into a fully featured project. This was also the largest project I had worked on at the time, requiring me to think more carefully about the overall architecture of the game.
I organized a team of friends from my game development club to work with me and it was up to us to push the project as far as we could. Since then, we have continued development and plan to release a beta version of the game later this year for the HTC Vive and later for Playstation VR.

Battle Of The Bards

HackPoly - 2017

Best Game Hack

2nd Place Overall

Previous Next

Battle Of The Bards was created in just 24 hours at the HackPoly 2017 hackathon, winning 'Best Game Hack' and '2nd Place Overall' vs 71 competing projects.

About the project
Tools: Unity, C#, GIT, Visual Studio

A clash between the rhythm and RPG genres, Battle of the Bards lets you fight an RPG boss battle with the power of rock! Players control a party of characters and cast spells from a deck of cards by playing rhythm game segments with a guitar controller or keyboard. Their performance determines the effectiveness of their spells.

Contributions

My contributions include:
  • Music generation system
  • Player UI and health/damage system
  • Turn based gameplay systems
  • Various spell mechanics
  • Various spell particle effects
  • Game Design (UI, player abilities, interactions)

I was fortunate to be able to work with 3 talented artists who created all original assets for our project. I learned a great deal from them about how to communicate effectively with artists including what tools they use, what technical details they need to know, and how to implement their designs accurately. Elemental Fury

BeachHacks - 2017

3rd Place Overall

Elemental Fury was created in just 24 hours at the BeachHacks 2017 hackathon, winning '3rd Place Overall' vs. 38 competing projects.

About the project
Tools: Unity, C#, GIT, Visual Studio, Photon, SteamVR, VRTK, Blender, HTC Vive

Elemental Fury is a multiplayer Virtual Reality battle arena game created for the HTC Vive. Players use hand motions to control the elements of fire, earth, air, and water. They must use these abilities to knock opposing players out of the arena, the last player standing is the winner. My inspiration for the game concept was the animated series Avatar and the bender battles from The Legend of Korra.

Contributions
I worked with two others engineers and a 3D artist to create Elemental Fury.
My contributions include:
  • Photon Unity Networking between multiple HTC Vives
  • Player controls for HTC Vive (using VRTK)
  • Earth and water element mechanics
  • Game Design (original concept, player abilities, interactions)
What I Learned

As this was my first networked game I spent most of the hackathon learning about the Photon Networking Framework. By the end of the hackathon I was able to achieve great synchronization between multiple Vive's using one or more sets of tracking modules. I also learned more about implementing VR player controls as our team designed creative ways to control the various elements in our game.

About the project
Tools: Unity, C#, GIT, Visual Studio, PlayMaker

Trnasfliction is a stealth/puzzle game where you play the villain. The player has infected themself with a deadly plague and must sneak past troublesome AI doctors in order to reach the center of the CDC and spread their disease to the masses. Seismic Shift

Global Game Jam - 2017

Previous Next

Seismic Shift was created in 36 hours during Global Game Jam 2017 at UC Irvine where the theme of the year was 'Waves'.

About the project
Tools: Unity, C#, GIT, Visual Studio

Seismic Shift is a unique take on the infinite runner genre. The player attempts to escape from a massive earthquake by driving their car through procedurally generated city roads. Seismic waves ripple down the streets, chaotic AI drivers flee for their lives, and meanwhile buildings collapse around you. It's your job to escape the disastrous earthquake with your life!

Contributions
I worked with a team of 2 other engineers and 2 game designers to create Seismic Shift.
My contributions include:
  • Procedural city generation system
  • Mesh-deforming wave generation system
  • Player vehicle controls
  • Game Design (original concept, mechanics, challenges)
What I Learned

This was my first time implementing either procedural generation or waves. I learned to implement a procedural generation system that links pre-built city blocks together before joining another engineer on my team who had been implementing a wave generation system using meshes. Our most difficult challenge was the mesh-deforming algorithm, which taught me a great deal about wave math and physics. I was also fortunate enough to work with 2 game design majors from UCI who helped me flesh out my orignal idea for the game, teaching me a lot more about game design in the process.
