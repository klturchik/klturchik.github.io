Red Dwarf

About the project

Red Dwarf is a virtual reality space shooter game developed for the HTC Vive. The player controls their spaceship from inside the cockpit, with VR-emulated flightstick controls. The game currently plays in an arcade-style, with the objective being to reach the highest score possible before dying.

Contribution

I worked with a team of 4 other engineers and a 3D artist to create Red Dwarf. As the team was very small we often shared responsibilities. My contributions include most of the design and implementation for the player UI and collision physics, as well as the implementation of player controls and weapon mechanics.

Accomplishments/Challenges

Our biggest challenge was making player controls feel intuitive and responsive while also alleviating motion sickness in VR. I am most proud of designing the radar which gives players 360 degree awareness of their surroundings.

What's next?

My team and I have been working to flesh out Red Dwarf with more weapons, enemy types, and missions. We are also currently experimenting with procedural generation of planets and debris. We hope to release an alpha version of the game some time in the next year.

    Note: Supports both HTC Vive as well as keyboard/mouse controls.

  • Tools: Unity, C#, Visual Studio, SteamVR, VRTK, HTC Vive
  • Date: May 2017

Battle Of The Bards

HackPoly - 2017

Best Game Hack

2nd Place Overall

Previous Next

Battle Of The Bards was originally created in just 24 hours at the HackPoly 2017 hackathon, winning 'Best Game Hack' and '2nd Place Overall' vs 71 competing projects.


About the project

A clash between the rhythm and RPG genres, Battle of the Bards lets you fight an RPG boss battle with the power of rock! Players control a party of characters and cast spells from a deck of cards by playing rhythm game segments with a guitar controller or keyboard. Their performance determines the effectiveness of the spells.

Contribution

I worked with another engineer and 2 artists to create Battle of the Bards. My contributions include most of the design and implementation for the player UI and spell mechanics.

Accomplishments/Challenges

Our biggest challenge was coordinating our team to work effectively during the 24 hour time frame. All of our art and code was original and created by our team during the 24 hour competition. I believe the fact that we were all good friends contributed to our success and ability to work together.

What's next?

Our team hopes to expand upon the system we created for Battle of the Bards to create a new full-fledged RPG system. I personally hope to release the game as a mobile app to make it easily accessible to as many people as possible.

    Note: Supports both USB Guitar Hero or Rock Band controllers as well as keyboard controls.

Elemental Fury

BeachHacks 2017

3rd Place Overall

Elemental Fury is a multiplayer Virtual Reality game created for the HTC Vive and inspired by the animated series, Avatar. Players use hand motions to control the elements of fire, earth, air, and water. They must use these abilities to knock other players out of the arena, the last player standing is the winner.

Elemental Fury was originally created in just 36 hours at the BeachHacks 2017 hackathon, and won '3rd Place Overall' vs. 38 competing projects.

  • Tools: Unity, C#, Visual Studio, SteamVR, VRTK, HTC Vive
  • Date: April 2017

InVRasion

Senior Project

Previous Next

InVRasion is a work in progress virtual reality game for the HTC Vive. It is an asynchronous multiplayer game in which a single VR player competes against up to four other players through co-operative splitscreen. The VR player plays as a giant, city destroying monster, while up to 4 other players play as different classes of super soldiers and attempt to defeat the monster before it destroys an objective.

InVRasion was originally developed as my Senior Project for my degree in Computer Science at Cal Poly Pomona. After receiving academic credit for InVRasion, development has continued as a hobby since January of 2017.

    Note: Requires an HTC Vive to play.

  • Tools: Unity, C#, Visual Studio, SteamVR, VRTK, HTC Vive
  • Date: September 2016

Seismic Shift

Global Game Jam - 2017

Previous Next

An Infinite Runner game themed around an earthquake destroying a city. Players drive a car through procedurally generated city roads attempting to survive. Seismic waves propagate down the roads, throwing everything in their path into the air.
