I'm an avid Software Engineer who has had a passion for programming and game development all my life.

With more than 5 years of experience creating games, I am eager to learn and try new approaches to implementing projects. I am proficient with Unity and Unreal Engine and have experimented extensively with VR development. I have attended a dozen hackathons and game jams where my teams' innovative games have often ranked amongst the top three projects overall.

Red Dwarf

Red Dwarf is an upcoming virtual reality space shooter game. The player controls their spaceship from inside the cockpit, with VR-emulated flightstick controls. The game currently plays in an arcade-style, with the objective being to reach as high of a score as possible before dying.

Red Dwarf was originally developed as the final project for my Game Development course at Cal Poly Pomona.

Battle Of The Bards

HackPoly - 2017

Best Game Hack

2nd Place Overall

Previous Next

A clash between Rhythm and RPG genres, this game lets you fight an RPG boss battle with the power of rock! Players fight a monster by using spell cards which are cast by playing rhythm game segments using a guitar controller or keyboard. Their song performance affects the strength of the spell.

Battle Of The Bards was created in just 24 hours at the HackPoly 2017 hackathon, and won 'Best Game Hack' vs 71 competing projects.

Created using Unity3D Game Engine and C#.

Note: Supports USB Guitar Hero or Rock Band controllers as well as keyboard controls.

Elemental Fury

BeachHacks 2017

3rd Place Overall

Elemental Fury is a multiplayer Virtual Reality game created for the HTC Vive and inspired by the animated series, Avatar. Players use hand motions to control the elements of fire, earth, air, and water. They must use these abilities to knock other player out of the arena, the last player standing is the winner.

Elemental Fury was created in just 36 hours at the BeachHacks 2017 hackathon, and won '3rd Place Overall' vs. 38 competing projects.

Created using Unity3D Game Engine and C#.

InVRasion

Senior Project

Previous Next

InVRasion is a work in progress virtual reality game for the HTC VIVE. It is an asynchronous multiplayer game in which a single VR player competes against up to four other players who use co-operative splitscreen. The VR player plays as a giant, city destroying monster, while the other players play as soldiers who attempt to defeat the monster before it destroys an objective.

InVRasion has been in development since January of 2017 as a Senior Project for my degree in Computer Science at Cal Poly Pomona. After receiving academic credit for InVRasion, development has continued as a hobby since March of 2017.

Created using Unity3D Game Engine and C#.

Note: Requires an HTC VIVE to play.

  • Tools: Unity, HTC Vive, SteamVR
  • Date: March 2017

Seismic Shift

Global Game Jam - 2017

Previous Next

An Infinite Runner game themed on an earthquake destroying a city. Players drive a car through procedurally generated city roads attempting to survive. Seismic waves propagate down the roads, throwing everything in its path into the air.

Seismic Shift was created in just 36 hours at Global Game Jam 2017 at UC Irvine. The theme of the game jam for this year was 'Waves'.

Created using Unity3D Game Engine and C#.
